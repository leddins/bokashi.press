What is Bokashi?

Bokashi is a method of organic waste fermentation that returns virtually all of the carbon back to the soil. The fermentation takes place in a sealed anaerobic environment and with the help of specialist microbes does not produce C02 or methane.

How to compost with bokashi

  1. Put a handful of inoculant flakes in the bottom of the bucket.
  2. Add kitchen scraps to the bucket, adding a handful of flakes for every few inches of depth. One gallon of flakes should be enough for one bucket.
  3. Use the plunger to press the food scraps down and keep out as much air as possible.
  4. Liquid will drip out and collect in the bottom bucket. If all is going well this should smell a little vinegary. Periodically pour it down the drain or dilute it and spread it on your garden.
  5. When the bucket is full, seal it with a lid and let it ferment for two weeks.
  6. After 2 weeks you can add it to regular compost or just put it directly in the ground.

Can I ferment that?

YES

  • Almost all kitchen scraps
  • Fruit and vegetables
  • Raw or cooked food
  • Grains and bread
  • Meat, fish, dairy, eggs
  • Small bones
  • Tea bags and coffee grounds
  • Tissues
  • Slightly moldy food

NO

  • Large quantities of liquids or oils
  • Thick bones and oyster shells
  • Very mold food
  • Paper
